# FASHION VIRUS — Dilemma cards (addendum for Karen)
### Cards with no clean answer

**Why these exist.** The seed corpus mostly trades money against morality — which players decode in three cards. These trade *values against values*: jobs vs rivers, autonomy vs welfare, carbon vs water, access vs purity. Some have only a best-worst option. On several, LEARN MORE makes the choice **harder**, not easier.

**The editing test (telemetry gives us this for free):** a dilemma card is working when player choices split somewhere near the middle. If 80%+ of players pick the same option, the card is a lecture wearing a costume — rewrite it. This is a measurable standard, and the Supabase logs will grade every card automatically.

Format as the main corpus: `V:` visible, `H:` hidden ledger.

---

**D1 · the_mill_town** — MANUFACTURING · from HIGH_STREET
Offer: Your longest-standing supplier — the dye house from your first collection — fails its effluent test badly. The village has 300 jobs and this mill. You are 60% of its order book.
A) Cut them off immediately → V: −£8k rev, sentiment +4 · H: river recovery begins; 300 livelihoods collapse, town enters decline (permanent world-state: mill_town_dies)
B) Stay and fund remediation over two years → V: −£45k cap, −£4k rev/turn for 6 turns · H: pollution continues, tapering; jobs survive · FUSE: toxic_discharge (reduced severity, but live — this can still blow up on you)
C) Quietly taper orders and say nothing → V: none now · H: pollution continues, town declines slowly · FUSE: supply_chain_opacity
DEPTH (1 turn): The mill owner shows you the books. Remediation genuinely works — but only if you commit publicly, which ties your brand to the pollution story if it fails. *(Depth raises the stakes; it does not tell you what to do.)*
ART: a terraced village street at dusk, mill chimney at its end, lights on in half the windows

**D2 · no_clean_fabric** — MATERIALS · from BEDROOM
Offer: The new line needs a base fabric. Three quotes on the desk. There is no fourth option.
A) Organic cotton → V: +6 heat, −£5k cap · H: 2.6M L water, 15 ha land in a drought-prone region
B) Recycled polyester → V: +3 heat · H: 500kg microplastics per season, forever
C) Conventional viscose → V: +£4k margin · H: 20 ha forest pressure, chemical processing
DEPTH: A lifecycle analyst's memo: "Each option externalises a different cost onto a different place. The question is not which is clean. The question is which harm you choose to own." *(Depth confirms there is no answer.)*
ART: three fabric swatches pinned side by side on a studio wall, morning light, entirely neutral

**D3 · the_overtime_petition** — MANUFACTURING · from HIGH_STREET
Offer: You move to cap hours at the factory after the deadline crunch. Li Pengjian brings you a petition — signed by 140 workers — *against* the cap. Peak-season overtime is school fees, medicine, remittances. "They are asking you not to protect them."
A) Impose the cap anyway → V: −£6k rev · H: welfare improves on paper; household incomes fall; trust with named characters drops (Yan Rong stops appearing in good-news whispers)
B) Keep overtime, add safeguards → V: +£8k rev, −£10k cap · H: excess hours continue, mitigated · FUSE: worker_grievance (low severity — safeguards can fail)
C) Raise base pay so overtime isn't needed → V: −£22k cap, −£5k rev/turn · H: genuinely better; margins now uncompetitive · adds novelty pressure +10 (you must sell your way out of the cost)
DEPTH: Yan Rong, privately: "The young ones want the hours. I am tired. Both things are true." *(Depth gives you two truths and no ruling.)*
ART: a folded paper petition on a workbench, many signatures visible as marks, not readable

**D4 · dirty_money_clean_purpose** — FUNDING · from HIGH_STREET
Offer: A fast-fashion conglomerate — everything you're supposedly against — offers £400k for a 10% stake in your repair-and-resale arm. Their money would take circular fashion mainstream through *their* 900 stores. Your name stays off it.
A) Take it → V: +£400k cap, resale scales nationally · H: circular volume ×20 — inside the machine that causes the problem; sentiment −8 if it surfaces · FUSE: greenwashing_claim (their behaviour, your exposure)
B) Decline → V: none · H: the conglomerate builds it anyway, worse, without you — world-state: circular_theatre (a hollow version exists at national scale, discrediting the idea)
DEPTH: Their sustainability director, off the record: "I have a real budget for exactly three years, then I'm replaced. Use me or don't." *(A genuine ally inside a dirty machine, on a clock.)*
ART: two coffee cups on a glass table between facing chairs, one branded lanyard just out of focus

**D5 · the_whistleblowers_choice** — MANUFACTURING · from GLOBAL
Offer: The worker who emailed about the single exit writes again. An audit is scheduled. If you act on her photograph, the subcontractor will know exactly who talked. She asks you to wait until she finds other work. The building is still the building.
A) Act now → V: −£40k cap, production halt · H: building made safe; she is identified and blacklisted from the sector (named character: permanent consequence)
B) Wait as she asks → V: none · H: risk continues each turn (probabilistic: fire fuse may fire catastrophically before she's clear)
C) Move her family onto your payroll first, then act → V: −£15k cap, costs 2 turns · H: risk continues 2 turns; she is protected — if the fuse doesn't fire first
DEPTH: The fire-safety engineer's report: "Probability of incident: low per month. Not zero. Cumulative." *(Depth quantifies the gamble; it cannot make it for you.)*
ART: an inbox on a dark screen, one unread message, cursor hovering — nothing else lit

**D6 · access_or_purity** — VOLUME · from HIGH_STREET
Offer: Your sustainable line works — at £120 a garment. A buyer for a value chain offers to license a simplified version at £15: heavier polyester blend, offshore production, but genuinely durable and repairable. "Sustainability that only the rich can buy isn't sustainability. It's positioning."
A) License it → V: +£30k rev/turn, share +8 · H: 200t CO₂/season, microplastics at scale — and 400,000 low-income customers get durable clothes that outlast six fast-fashion cycles (net ledger: genuinely ambiguous, and the game should show both columns)
B) Refuse; protect the standard → V: sentiment +6 among your customers · H: the value chain fills the shelf with a 6-wash garment instead; total system waste *rises* (world-state: someone_worse)
DEPTH: Karen's-research card: lifecycle data showing the £15 durable blend, worn for years, can out-perform the £120 organic piece worn twice. Ownership behaviour, not fibre, dominates the footprint. *(Depth actively argues for the "dirty" option.)*
ART: two identical white shirts on one rail, price tags turned away

**D7 · the_recall** — END_OF_LIFE · from HIGH_STREET
Offer: A dye batch in 60,000 shipped garments shows trace contamination — well under the legal limit, above your own published standard. Nobody outside the lab knows. A recall is ruinous; silence is a bet.
A) Full public recall → V: −£120k cap, −15 heat, sentiment +10 *later* if you frame it honestly · H: 60k garments destroyed: 180t CO₂, 45t waste — the recall itself is an environmental event
B) Quietly fix the process, say nothing → V: none · H: contamination is real but sub-legal; trust debt · FUSE: greenwashing_claim (your own standard, weaponised)
C) Offer voluntary returns without stating why → V: −£30k cap, −4 heat · H: partial: a third come back; the rest are worn
DEPTH: Toxicologist: "At these levels, risk is negligible. Reputationally, 'negligible' has never once survived a headline." *(The safe choice for people and the safe choice for the world point in opposite directions: the recall protects trust by generating waste.)*
ART: a single folded garment in a specimen bag on a lab bench, fluorescent light

**D8 · triage** — ACT 2 · CHANGE path
Offer: Restoration budget, one year. The engineers are unanimous: it funds one project properly or two badly.
A) The river → H: bioremediation begins; the mill town's water is safe in ~8 turns; the landfill continues leaching
B) The landfill cap → H: leaching stops; the meadow becomes possible; the river stays dead another decade
C) Split it → H: both projects start; both stall at 40%; neither completes this playthrough *(the engineers said this; the game lets you not listen)*
DEPTH: The hydrologist's note: "Whichever you choose, choose. Half-funded remediation is how sites stay broken for fifty years." *(Depth begs you to accept the dilemma rather than dodge it — the dodge is the worst option, and it will be the most popular.)*
ART: two site maps side by side on a folding table in a portacabin, mud on the floor

## Editing principles these cards follow (for writing more)

1. **Two goods or two harms — never good vs greed.** If one option is "be decent" and the other is "be paid," bin it.
2. **Depth complicates.** LEARN MORE should sometimes make the reader *less* comfortable. Information is not absolution.
3. **Someone sympathetic disagrees with the virtuous move.** Petitions, whistleblowers with their own plans, allies inside dirty machines.
4. **Refusal has consequences too.** Declining doesn't freeze the world — someone worse moves in. No clean-hands exit.
5. **Probabilistic fuses.** Some risks should be dice, not certainties — betting and losing feels different from choosing badly, and both belong in the game.
6. **World-states, not just numbers.** mill_town_dies, someone_worse, circular_theatre — some choices change the *map*, and Act 2 should show it.
7. **The telemetry verdict:** near-50% splits = dilemma. 80%+ consensus = lecture. Rewrite or cut.
