# Card difficulty review — hardening the 30 seeds

A review of `fashion-virus-30-cards.md` against the editing principles in
`fashion-virus-dilemma-cards.md` ("two goods or two harms — never good vs greed";
depth complicates; someone sympathetic disagrees; refusal has consequences;
probabilistic fuses; world-states; the telemetry verdict).

D1–D7 are now in the deck. The telemetry view `card_choice_splits` grades every
card automatically once players exist: near-50% split = dilemma, 80%+ = lecture.

---

## Verdicts on the original 30

### Already hard enough — leave alone
- **02 leather** and **03 polyester** are proto-D2s: three-way harm trades (cost vs carbon vs plastic vs water). Genuine dilemmas.
- **07 deadstock**, **09B tea**, **30 rental** — the deliberate "decency pays" cards. They *should* be one-sided; they're calibration, not dilemmas.
- **08 offshore** (the inspect option), **12 audit**, **18 hedge fund**, **20 covenant**, **26 documentary** — structural cards. Their job is teaching the machine, not splitting the room.
- **14 novelty**, **22 greenwash**, **28 returns** — the trap and the lever. Lopsided splits here are *data*, not failure: if 85% take free returns, that's the finding.

### Lectures wearing costumes — harden these

**01 · dye_signature_colour.** Money+heat vs slightly-less is good-vs-greed.
*Harden:* the crimson **is** the brand — option B should cost identity, not just margin.
Add: "The chalky alternative photographs like everyone else's spring line" (novelty −8),
and a whisper on B: *"A competitor ships something crimson in six weeks. It isn't as good. It doesn't matter."*
Refusal has consequences; the colour happens either way.

**04 · viscose_forest.** Clean option is strictly virtuous at a modest price.
*Harden:* lyocell has a 10-week lead time — take it and miss the season (novelty −12).
Now it's forest vs momentum, not good vs cheap.

**05 · sandblasted_denim.** Silicosis vs £24k is no contest once you look.
*Harden (D1-style):* the finishing house is a family firm; insist on laser and they
lose the contract entirely — 60 jobs — because the machinery loan needs your volume
committed for three years (a covenant-shaped string). Lungs vs livelihoods vs lock-in.

**06 · angora_moment.** Declining is a clean-hands exit (violates principle 4).
*Harden:* decline and the trend happens anyway — plant `someone_worse`, and the
lambswool option cannibalises your own knitwear margin. Also a candidate for a
**probabilistic fuse**: the footage surfaces on a dice roll, not a certainty —
betting and losing feels different from choosing badly.

**10 · the_deadline.** Money-vs-morality with a safe middle option.
*Harden:* link it to D3's insight — the factory *wants* the overtime (it's the
year's bonus). Refusing the deadline costs the workers the peak-season pay AND
loses you the account. The middle option should annoy everyone slightly.

**13 · factory_fire_warning.** Obvious morality (which is fine — it's a setup card).
*Note:* D5 is its sequel. In the full game, gate D5 on 13A having been chosen
(engine needs card prerequisites — small addition). The pair is stronger than either card.

**15 · the_collab.** Declining costs nothing.
*Harden:* the streetwear label is a two-person outfit and this is their break.
Decline and they fold; do the drop and you're selling engineered scarcity.
Someone sympathetic is on the wrong side of the virtuous move.

**16 · size_run_gamble.** Inclusion vs margin is a lecture in 2026.
*Harden into values-vs-values:* the extended range's fit data is poor — returns
run double, and returns are landfill (per card 28). Inclusion vs waste. Real, uncomfortable, current.

**19 · growth_pressure.** Truth-telling is obviously right and merely expensive.
*Harden:* refusing 40% triggers a world-state — `founder_sidelined`: the board
installs a "commercial co-CEO" and later cards' dirty options become the *default*
unless you spend cash to overrule. Refusal doesn't freeze the world; it moves the world into other hands.

**24 · the_takedown.** Suppressing a journalist is cartoon-evil.
*Harden:* the piece is 80% right but conflates your subcontractor with a worse one —
responding honestly means publicly confirming the true 80%. Silence protects a
falsehood that favours you. (Information is not absolution.)

**27 · unsold_stock.** Incineration vs discount vs recycling — the depth settles it.
*Harden:* make **donation** a real fourth option that feeds the Ghana bale-market
problem (ties to 29 and the A4 bale-port art). All four options now harm differently:
burn (carbon), discount (brand—and the volume system that made 40k units), donate
(someone else's country), recycle (a fraction is recyclable; the rest is theatre).

**29 · the_export_offer.** Signing is cartoonishly bad.
*Harden with the real-world contest:* the broker genuinely supplies affordable
clothing markets — ban exports and those markets lose supply; a portion IS worn
for years. Make it D6's sibling: access vs waste, with depth citing both columns.

## New dilemma card ideas (in the D-format)

**N1 · the_interns_thread** — MARKETING · HIGH_STREET
Your own junior posts an accurate, critical thread about the industry — including you. It's going viral, and so is the pile-on against her.
A) Legal takedown → suppression fuse, she's protected from the mob by erasure
B) Amplify it yourself → heat −10 now, trust later; she becomes a target with your reach attached
C) Say nothing → she faces the pile-on alone; duty of care vs brand, unresolved
DEPTH: she DMs you: "Please don't make this about the brand." *(Both action options do.)*

**N2 · the_counterfeit_compliment** — GLOBAL
Counterfeits of your bestseller flood the market — made, your agent suspects, by your own factory's overflow units.
A) Enforce → lawyers crush micro-workshops employing hundreds
B) Ignore → unverifiable garments with your name on them (safety, dye standards — the recall problem at one remove)
C) License the workshops cheaply → legitimise and inspect them; the board calls it rewarding theft
*(Autonomy vs welfare vs control; there is no option where nobody loses.)*

**N3 · the_water_table** — MATERIALS · GLOBAL
Your organic cotton region enters long-term drought. Farmers converted three years ago on your word and your premiums.
A) Switch regions → saves water, abandons the people who trusted you (world-state: broken_word)
B) Stay → loyalty, at 2.6M litres a season from a falling aquifer
C) Fund drip irrigation → −£60k, works in 4 turns — if the aquifer lasts that long (probabilistic)
*(Loyalty vs hydrology. Depth: the hydrologist and the farmers' co-op disagree, both correctly.)*

**N4 · the_living_wage_ledger** — MANUFACTURING · GLOBAL
You move your main factory to a living wage. Other brands pull orders — you made the factory expensive. Volume falls 40%; total worker income *drops*.
A) Fill the book yourself → you must grow to justify decency (the trap, inverted)
B) Accept the shrinkage → the wage is real; the jobs are fewer
C) Revert quietly → whisper: "Nobody thanks you for the three months it lasted."
*(Decency creates a growth compulsion — Wiener again, from the virtuous side.)*

**N5 · lenas_collapse** — MARKETING · HIGH_STREET
Lena — the face of the campaign since the bedroom years — burns out publicly, mid-campaign, in a way that is generating enormous engagement.
A) Pull the campaign → protects her; kills the drop and the junior team's bonuses
B) Run it → the numbers are extraordinary and she is in a clinic
C) Pause and donate the slot to her statement → she didn't ask for that either
DEPTH: her manager: "She'd tell you to run it. That's the illness talking. Or it's her. I don't know."

**N6 · the_insurance_burn** — END_OF_LIFE · HIGH_STREET
Flood-damaged warehouse. The insurer pays full value only if stock is destroyed (brand-protection clause). Donating the wearable half voids the payout — which is this quarter's payroll.
A) Burn it, take the payout → payroll made; 20k wearable garments destroyed
B) Donate, eat the loss → someone misses payroll; Priya-tier staff first
*(Money vs waste with named people on the money side.)*

## D8 / Act 2 (not yet implemented)

D8 (triage) is the right shape for the **full** Act 2: one budget, mutually
exclusive repairs, and the split-it option that stalls both (which telemetry
predicts will be the most popular — that's the point). The current PoC Act 2 is
a flat repair menu; implementing D8 properly needs repair prerequisites and
multi-year projects. Recommend it as the first Act 2 feature after the PoC —
it converts Act 2 from a menu into a dilemma.

Also for the full game: **card prerequisites** (13 → D5 chaining), **probabilistic
fuses** (a `risk` field: fires on a dice roll per turn rather than on threshold),
and world-states that alter Act 2's *scene selection*, not just its text.
